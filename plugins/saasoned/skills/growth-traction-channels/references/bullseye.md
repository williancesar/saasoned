# Bullseye Framework

**Definition.** A repeatable method to find the one distribution channel that
works: brainstorm every channel → rank into three concentric rings → run cheap
parallel tests on the inner ring → focus the single channel that produces a
traction signal → exploit it until it tops out, then repeat.

**Source.** Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*.

**When it applies.** Any product with no working channel; flat growth; "what's
our marketing plan"; deciding whether to add/switch channels.

**When it does NOT.** When a channel is already working and not yet saturated —
don't re-run Bullseye out of boredom; focus is the point. Also not the tool for
"is the problem real" (discovery) or "what is this" (positioning).

## The four/five steps in detail

1. **Brainstorm (outer thinking).** For *each* of the 19 channels, write at
   least one concrete, product-specific idea. The discipline is generating an
   idea even for channels you "know" won't work — that bias is exactly what
   makes founders miss their actual best channel.
2. **Rank into rings.**
   - **Inner ring (3):** worth a real test right now — plausible reach to the
     beachhead, affordable, fast to a signal.
   - **Middle ring:** promising but not yet — keep warm.
   - **Outer ring:** unlikely; park.
   Rank with the beachhead in mind (see `growth-positioning`), not a generic
   audience.
3. **Test the inner ring cheaply and in parallel.** Each test is the smallest
   spend that answers: approximate CAC here, volume of reachable *right* users,
   and quality. Time-boxed (days to ~2 weeks), with a pre-stated kill condition.
4. **Focus the ONE.** When a test shows a traction signal, stop the others and
   concentrate all effort on optimizing that single channel. Per the
   distribution power law, one channel typically dominates; running several
   dilutes the winner.
5. **Iterate the channel, not the framework.** Optimize the focused channel
   until it saturates or its economics break; only then re-brainstorm.

## Decision cue

```
No working channel?            → full Bullseye pass (all 19 → 3 inner → test)
Inner-ring test shows signal?  → drop the rest, focus that one
Focused channel saturating?    → re-run Bullseye for the next one
Tempted to run 4+ at once?     → STOP: distribution power law; pick one
No signal anywhere?            → revisit positioning/discovery, not more channels
```

## Worked micro-example

*vai-nascer-v2*: inner ring = **Content Marketing** (trimester-by-trimester
guides ranking for pregnancy-stage searches), **Community Building** (presence
in Brazilian pregnancy forums), **Existing Platforms** (integration/visibility
in a widely used pregnancy-tracking ecosystem). Three two-week tests, each with
a kill condition ("<X qualified installs from the right segment → kill"). Focus
the one that pulls first-trimester pregnant women who then add family members.

## Common failure it prevents

Running many half-funded channels forever, never giving any one enough focus to
reveal whether it could have dominated — the single most common traction death.
