# The 19 Traction Channels

**Source.** Weinberg & Mares, *Traction*. The thesis: most founders default to
the one or two channels they already know; the 19-channel catalog forces
consideration of all of them so the actual best channel isn't missed.

**Use.** During Bullseye step 1, generate one concrete, product-specific idea
for **every** channel below. Do not skip a channel because it "feels wrong" —
that bias is the point of the catalog. Each entry: what it is · a fitting cue ·
a watch-out.

| # | Channel | What it is | Fits when | Watch-out |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Viral / Targeting Blogs** | Get covered by niche blogs your users read | A defined niche with active blogs | Tiny blogs ≠ scale; pick ones with the right readers |
| 2 | **Publicity (PR)** | Press/media coverage | A genuine story or news hook | Spiky, not durable; rarely a standalone channel |
| 3 | **Unconventional PR** | Stunts, outsized customer appreciation | Small budget, shareable gesture | Hard to repeat; novelty decays |
| 4 | **SEM** | Paid search ads | Existing search demand for the problem | Dead zone if CAC > margin; needs intent |
| 5 | **Social & Display Ads** | Paid social/display | Targetable audience, brand/demand creation | Easy to burn cash with no signal |
| 6 | **Offline Ads** | TV, radio, print, billboards | Broad or local mass audience, later stage | Poor early-stage measurability |
| 7 | **SEO** | Rank in organic search | Recurring search intent, content capacity | Slow; compounding, not a quick test |
| 8 | **Content Marketing** | Owned content that attracts/educates | Teachable domain, audience that reads | Long payback; needs distribution itself |
| 9 | **Email Marketing** | Lifecycle/nurture/retention email | You can capture addresses; repeat value | Acquisition-weak; mostly retention |
| 10 | **Engineering as Marketing** | Free tools/widgets/calculators that draw users | You can ship a useful micro-tool | Tool must map to the core product's user |
| 11 | **Viral Marketing** | Users recruit users | Built-in reason to invite; k-factor > 0 | Don't fake it; needs real loop |
| 12 | **Business Development** | Partnerships that route users to you | Partners already hold your audience | Slow deals; misaligned incentives |
| 13 | **Sales** | Direct selling | High order value, complex/considered purchase | Too costly below the dead-zone line |
| 14 | **Affiliate Programs** | Pay third parties per result | Clear payout economics, CLV headroom | Fraud/quality risk; needs margin |
| 15 | **Existing Platforms** | Ride a big platform (app store, marketplace, social graph) | A platform concentrates your users | Platform risk; rules change |
| 16 | **Trade Shows** | Industry expos | B2B/vertical with show culture | Expensive; weak for pure consumer |
| 17 | **Offline Events** | Meetups, conferences you run/attend | Local density, community product | Doesn't scale linearly |
| 18 | **Speaking Engagements** | Talks to gathered target audiences | You have authority/story; niche events | Reach-limited per event |
| 19 | **Community Building** | Cultivate an owned community | Shared identity/passion among users | Slow to seed; high trust payoff |

## Decision cue

For each channel ask: *Are the right users reachable here in volume, at a cost
below their lifetime value, fast enough to get a signal in ~2 weeks?* Yes →
inner-ring candidate. Maybe-later → middle. No → outer. Never "skip."

## Worked micro-example

*family-agent*: don't stop at "ads + app store." Forced ideas surface
**Engineering as Marketing** (free shareable "family logistics load" calculator)
and **Community Building** (parenting coordination communities) — often stronger
for a consumer utility than paid social, and only found because the catalog
forbade skipping.

## Common failure it prevents

Defaulting to the founder's familiar 1–2 channels and never discovering the
channel that would actually have dominated.
