# Variable Rewards — Tribe / Hunt / Self

**Source.** Nir Eyal, *Hooked* (the Variable Reward stage).

**Definition.** The reward that resolves the trigger's craving must be
**variable** — its presence, magnitude, or form is uncertain. Predictable
rewards lose their grip; variability sustains engagement. Three reward classes:

- **Rewards of the Tribe** — social payoff: validation, belonging, reciprocity,
  cooperation, competition (likes, replies, status, a teammate's reaction).
- **Rewards of the Hunt** — pursuit of resources/information: money, deals, a
  feed of novel content, search results, useful tips.
- **Rewards of the Self** — intrinsic mastery/completion: progress, control,
  competence, finishing, collecting, "I did it."

**When it applies.** Designing or auditing the reward stage; "the reward isn't
working / engagement is flat despite a reward."

**When it does NOT.** When the underlying value is absent — a variable reward
on a worthless action is a slot machine, not retention; that's a Manipulation-
Matrix problem (see manipulation-matrix.md).

**Decision cue.**
1. Is the reward *variable* (not a fixed, predictable payout)? If no → it won't
   form a habit.
2. Does the reward *type* match the internal trigger? (Loneliness → tribe;
   uncertainty/seeking → hunt; need for competence/closure → self.) Mismatched
   type → weak loop.
3. Is the variability honest (real value, sometimes more) rather than
   artificial scarcity/loss-framing? Artificial → Matrix risk.

**Worked micro-example.** *vai-nascer-v2* weekly check resolves the internal
trigger "is this normal?" → matched reward is **self** (reassurance/competence:
"this week is on track") with a **tribe** layer if family reacts. A points
badge (predictable, mismatched) would be the wrong reward and form no habit.

**Common failure it prevents.** Bolting a generic gamification reward (fixed
points/badges) onto a loop where it is both predictable and mismatched to the
trigger — engagement theater that never becomes a habit.
