# Growth: Customer Discovery

Owns truthful learning and audience-building. Hub: `growth-foundations`
(worldview, shared vocabulary, router). Does not redefine hub terms.

**Stance.** Talk about their life, not your idea. Opinions, compliments, and
hypotheticals are **bad data** and must be excluded — not softened. Find your
audience *before/while* building; "build it and they will come" is a predicted
failure (hub Guardrail), not a strategy.

## Question hygiene (the core filter)

Good data is **specifics about the past and actual behavior**. Bad data is
**generics, opinions, and hypotheticals about the future**. Apply on every
question:

| Bad (exclude/rewrite) | Good (seek) |
|---|---|
| "Would you use…?" / "Do you think…?" | "Walk me through the last time you…" |
| "Do you like this idea?" | "What have you tried to solve it? What did it cost you?" |
| "Would you pay for X?" | "What do you pay for today to deal with this?" |
| Any compliment received | A commitment of time/reputation/money |

Default move: **convert every hypothetical/opinion question into a
past-behavior question** before the conversation.

## Commitment & advancement (did you actually learn anything?)

A conversation only counts as validation if the person spends a **currency** and
**advances**:

- **Currencies:** time (a real next meeting/work), reputation (an intro, a
  public endorsement), money (pre-order, deposit, paid pilot).
- **Advancement:** they move to a concrete next step. Compliments and "keep me
  posted" are *zero-commitment* → no signal.

Decision cue: after each conversation, name the currency spent and the next
step secured. None → you got bad data, regardless of enthusiasm.

## Audience-first loop (Embedded Entrepreneur)

```
Audience Discovery  →  Audience Exploration  →  Audience Transformation
(find where they      (embed; learn their      (build the product the
 already gather)        language & problems)     audience pulls you toward)
```

Embed in an existing community of the audience and earn standing *before*
pitching anything. Building in public turns the audience into co-creators and a
distribution channel. If the audience is unknown → start here before positioning
or channels.

## Decision cues

- **"Validate my idea"** → don't. Validate the *problem* via past behavior; the
  idea is not the subject of the conversation.
- **"They loved it!"** → compliment = bad data. Ask what they *did* / will
  commit. Deflect the compliment, dig for specifics.
- **"Who do we even build for?"** → Audience Discovery first; positioning and
  channels depend on a known audience.
- **Interview script provided** → run the critique-and-rewrite procedure
  ([references/question-rewrite.md](references/question-rewrite.md)).

## Anti-patterns (this skill's catalog)

1. **Interviews-as-pitches** (hub anti-pattern 6) — talking about your idea,
   collecting compliments, founder talks more than the user.
2. **Hypothetical/leading questions** treated as evidence ("would you…?").
3. **Compliments as validation** — enthusiasm without a spent currency.
4. **Skipping audience discovery** — building, then hunting for who wants it
   ("build it and they will come").
5. **Asking for opinions on the solution** instead of facts about the problem.
6. **No advancement** — every conversation ends with "great, keep me posted."

## Worked example (illustrative — not a product spec)

*family-agent* bad question: "Would you use an app that coordinates your
family?" (hypothetical → bad data). Rewritten: "Tell me about the last time
something fell through the cracks coordinating the family — what happened, what
did you do, what did it cost you?" Commitment test: ask for a 30-min follow-up
with their partner present; a yes (time currency + advancement) is signal, "love
it, keep me posted" is not.
